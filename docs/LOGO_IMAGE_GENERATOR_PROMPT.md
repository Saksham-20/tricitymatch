## Full prompt (copy-paste)


Logo design for "TricityShadi", a matrimonial (marriage and life-partner matchmaking) platform for the Tricity region of India: Chandigarh, Mohali, and Panchkula. Take inspiration from established Indian matrimonial websites (e.g. Shaadi.com, BharatMatrimony, Jeevansathi, Matrimony.com) and use them as reference for tone, symbolism, and trust—while staying distinct and modern. The logo must feel elegant, trustworthy, and romantic but dignified—suitable for families and serious commitment, not casual dating.

Concept to convey:
- "Tricity" = three cities unified; suggest three elements (e.g. three shapes, three peaks, three leaves, or a triad motif) that read as one cohesive mark.
- Connection and partnership: two people, two hearts, or a couple coming together (subtle, symbolic—e.g. interlocking shapes, two curves meeting, or a single heart with a "match" or "link" idea).
- Indian matrimony: take visual and symbolic inspiration from Indian matrimonial websites (trust marks, union motifs, traditional-meets-modern balance). Optional subtle cultural touch (e.g. delicate paisley, bangle, or traditional motif) without looking clichéd or heavy. Can be minimal or omitted for a more modern look.
- Trust and permanence: clean, balanced, timeless shape that works at small sizes (favicon, app icon) and large (hero, print).

Color palette (use these exactly or very close):
- Primary: deep burgundy rose (#8B2346) or rich rose (#E11D48)—main symbol or dominant shape.
- Accent: royal gold (#C9A227)—secondary detail, highlight, or small element.
- Optional: soft blush pink (#F8E8EC or #FDF2F5) for a gentle gradient or background only if the logo is shown on a tinted background.
- For a single-color or dark version: the mark should work in solid burgundy/rose on white, and in white or light pink on dark burgundy.

Style:
- Modern, refined, slightly premium ("royal elegance")—avoid cartoon, casual, or playful.
- Prefer a symbol or icon that can stand alone (no wordmark in the image is fine; the word "TricityShadi" can be added in design software).
- Clean lines, simple shapes; readable and recognizable at 32×32 px and 512×512 px.
- Flat design or very subtle gradient; no heavy 3D, gloss, or noisy texture.
- No literal rings, wedding cakes, or overly literal marriage clichés unless integrated in a minimal, abstract way.

Output:
- Square or horizontal logo mark (symbol only), transparent or white background, centered, no extra text or watermarks.
- Format: suitable for favicon, app icon, navbar, and marketing—professional and memorable.

## Tips for best results

1. **Reference Indian matrimonial sites:** Look at Shaadi.com, BharatMatrimony, Jeevansathi, Matrimony.com for logo style, color use (reds, golds, serif wordmarks), and the balance of traditional symbolism with clean, professional marks. Use them as inspiration, not copy.
2. **Iterate:** Run the prompt 2–3 times; pick the clearest, most scalable mark.
3. **Add tool-specific suffixes** if needed:
   - Midjourney: `--v 6 --style raw` or `--s 100` for simpler shapes.
   - Ideogram: add "logo, vector style, simple shapes".
   - DALL·E: you can add "minimal vector logo, transparent background".
4. **After generation:** Export as PNG with transparency (or SVG if the tool supports it), then add the wordmark "TricityShadi" in Figma/Canva/Illustrator using Playfair Display or a similar serif.
5. **Versions to create:** (1) Full color symbol, (2) Burgundy-only on white, (3) White on burgundy for dark headers/footers.

## What to avoid in the logo

- Casual or dating-app look (hearts with arrows, wink emoji, etc.).
- Too many elements (keep to one clear idea: Tricity + connection).
- Literal wedding props (rings, cakes, bouquets) unless very abstract.
- Cluttered or tiny details that disappear at 32px.
- Generic "tech" or "social app" icons with no link to matrimony or Tricity.
